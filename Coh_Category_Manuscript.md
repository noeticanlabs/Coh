## Abstract
We introduce the **Coh category**, a verifier-indexed, constraint-enriched categorical structure in which morphisms are generated by admissibility rather than assumed a priori. We prove that verifier-certified transitions form a category under telescoping inequality closure, and that projection-induced information loss yields an oplax composition law. We further demonstrate that bicategorical structure arises naturally from slack in composition, where 2-cells explicitly represent the optimization gap between syntactic operational ledgers and semantic physical bounds.

## 1. Introduction
Traditional category theory assumes morphisms exist independently of constraints. In real systems, transitions must satisfy verification and resource constraints. 

> **Morphisms exist if and only if they are verifier-admissible.**

Unlike enriched categories, the Coh framework generates morphisms from the admissibility verification itself, isolating physical constraints into a verifiable ledger.

## 2. Coh Systems
### 2.1 Definition (Coh System)
A Coh System is a tuple $\mathcal{S} = (X, \mathcal{K}, V, \Pi, \text{RV})$ where:
- $X$ is the state space.
- $\mathcal{K}$ is the set of admissible states.
- $V$ is a violation functional.
- $\Pi$ is the observation projection.
- $\text{RV}$ is the receipt verifier.

### 2.2 Hidden State
Each observable state $x$ corresponds to a fiber of hidden microscopic encodings $h_x \in \mathcal{H}$, such that $\Pi(h_x) = x$.

## 3. Admissibility and The Verifier
### 3.1 Admissible Transition
A transition morphism $r: x \to x'$ exists if and only if $\text{RV}(x, r, x') = \text{ACCEPT}$ and:
$$V(x') + s(r) \le V(x) + d(r)$$
where $s(r)$ is the macroscopic spend and $d(r)$ is the allowed defect.

---

## 4. Projection and The Oplax Envelope
### 4.1 Envelope Cost
The minimal safe macroscopic spend $s(R)$ must satisfy the supremum over the hidden fiber:
$$s(R) \ge \sup_{\Psi \in \mathcal{F}_R} W(\Psi)$$

### 4.2 Oplax Bound (Proposition)
The macroscopic spend functional is strictly subadditive (oplax) under composition:
$$s(r_2 \circ r_1) \le s(r_1) + s(r_2)$$

## 5. The Coh Category
### 5.1 Composition Semantics
- **Syntactic Composition**: Raw concatenation of receipts ($s_{\text{syn}} = s_1 + s_2$), preserving strict associativity.
- **Semantic Composition**: The tightest possible bound ($s_{\text{sem}} \le s_{\text{syn}}$).

---

## 6. Trace Algebra
### 6.1 Telescoping Trace Closure (Theorem)
For any finite chain of admissible transitions $x_0 \xrightarrow{r_1} x_1 \dots \xrightarrow{r_n} x_n$:
$$V(x_n) + \sum_{i=1}^n s_i \le V(x_0) + \sum_{i=1}^n d_i$$
This ensures that evaluating the endpoints securely bounds the global behavior.

## 7. Locally Posetal 2-Category Structure
### 7.1 Slack 2-Cell
A 2-cell $f \xrightarrow{\Delta} g$ exists if $g.d = f.d + \Delta$ for $\Delta \ge 0$. The hom-sets form a partial order based on defect bounds.

### 7.2 Semantic Initial Property
The tightest possible receipt (Semantic Bound) is the **initial object** in the poset of valid receipts, with a canonical 2-cell $\text{sem} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{syn}$.

---

## 8. Separation Law
The spend functional and the defect allowance are structurally independent: $s(r) \not\equiv d(r)$.

## 9. Worked Example
For a progression $x_0 \to x_1 \to x_2$ with $V(x_0) = 10$, $s_1=2, d_1=1$ and $s_2=3, d_2=1$:
- $V(x_1) \le 9$.
- $V(x_2) \le 7$.
- Global check: $V(x_2) + 5 \le 12 \implies V(x_2) \le 7$.
